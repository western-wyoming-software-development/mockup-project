Time Machine Product Requirements Document

1. Overview

This document outlines the key requirements and features for the development of a prototype time machine. The primary goal is to design and build a machine capable of transporting users through time while ensuring maximum safety and ease of use.

2. Objectives

  • Enable users to select specific time destinations (past or future).
  • Ensure user safety during temporal displacement.
  • Allow for precise navigation to historical or futuristic points.
  • Log all time-travel sessions for documentation and troubleshooting.

3. Functional Requirements

3.1 User Interface (UI)

  • Touchscreen display for date/time input, including options for year, month, day, hour, and minute.
  • Display panel for real-time travel status and system diagnostics.
  • Confirmation screen to review destination details before initiating travel.

3.2 Time Travel Mechanism

  • Quantum displacement engine to facilitate temporal shifts.
  • Integrated stabilizers to ensure smooth transitions and prevent spatial dislocation.
  • Emergency override button for immediate return to present time.

3.3 Safety Features

  • Automatic shutdown upon detecting any structural or environmental risks.
  • Helmet with integrated sensors to monitor user vitals during travel.
  • Alarm system for any discrepancies in timeline compatibility.

4. Non-Functional Requirements

  • Reliability: 99.99% uptime, ensuring minimal downtime for maintenance and repairs.
  • Scalability: Capability to support multiple users with simultaneous trips by creating separate timelines.
  • Security: Encryption of all travel logs and data to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Usability: Intuitive controls for ease of use, even for first-time users.

5. Constraints

  • Limited to human time travelers only (no inanimate objects or animals).
  • Restricted travel to dates within Earth’s recorded history and estimated future range of 3000 years.
  • Complies with known laws of physics, with focus on theoretical quantum mechanics.

6. Dependencies

  • Quantum displacement engine prototype.
  • Advanced materials for structural durability during temporal shifts.
  • Collaboration with historical and theoretical physicists for timeline integrity.

7. Milestones

  • Phase 1: Research and development of quantum displacement engine – 6 months.
  • Phase 2: Prototype build and initial testing – 12 months.
  • Phase 3: Beta testing with limited user group – 3 months.
  • Phase 4: Public release and operational deployment – 6 months.
